Bias-Variance decomposition

$$y=f(x)+\epsilon,\ \epsilon \sim N(0,\sigma^2)$$

$$E[(y-\hat{f(x)})^2]=(Bias[\hat{f(x)}])^2+Var[\hat{f(x)}]+\sigma^2$$

$$E[(y-\hat{f})^2]=E[(f+\epsilon-\hat{f})^2]$$

$$=E[(f+\epsilon-\hat{f}+E[\hat{f}]-E[\hat{f}])^2]$$

$$=E[(f-E[\hat{f}])^2]+E[(E[\hat{f}]-\hat{f})^2]+E[\epsilon^2]$$

$$+2E[(f-E[\hat{f}])\epsilon]+2E[\epsilon(E[\hat{f}]-\hat{f}])]+2E[(E[\hat{f}]-\hat{f} )(f-E[\hat{f}])]$$


$$=(f-E[\hat{f}])^2+E[\epsilon^2]+E[(E[\hat{f}]-\hat{f})^2]$$

$$+2(f-E[\hat{f}])E[\epsilon]+2E[\epsilon]E[E[\hat{f}]-\hat{f}]+2E[E[\hat{f}]-\hat{f}](f-E[\hat{f}])$$

$$=(f-E[\hat{f}])^2+Var[y]+Var[\hat{f}]$$

$$=Bias[\hat{f}]^2+\sigma^2+Var[\hat{f}]$$
